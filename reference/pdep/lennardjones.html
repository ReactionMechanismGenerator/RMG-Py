rmgpy.pdep.LennardJones¶

class rmgpy.pdep.LennardJones¶

LennardJones(sigma=None, epsilon=None)

A set of Lennard-Jones collision parameters. The attributes are:

Attribute Description
sigma Distance at which the inter-particle potential is minimum
epsilon Depth of the potential well

The Lennard-Jones collision model is based on the empirical potential function

\[V(r) = 4 \epsilon \left[ \left( \frac{\sigma}{r} \right)^{12} - \left( \frac{\sigma}{r} \right)^{6} \right]\]

where \(\epsilon\) is the depth of the potential well and \(\sigma\) is related to the distance at which the potential is minimized by \(r_\mathrm{m} = 2^{1/6} \sigma\). The collision frequency in the Lennard-Jones model is given by

\[\omega(T,[\mathrm{M}]) = \Omega_i^{(2,2)\ast} \sqrt{\frac{8 k_\mathrm{B} T}{\pi \mu}} \pi \sigma^2 [\mathrm{M}]\]

where \(T\) is temperature, \([\mathrm{M}]\) is the collider concentration, and \(\mu\) is the reduced mass. The configurational integral \(\Omega^{(2,2)*}\) is well-approximated by a simple algebraic equation in temperature

\[\Omega_i^{(2,2)\ast} = 1.16145 \tilde{T}^{-0.14874} + 0.52487 e^{-0.7732 \tilde{T}} + 2.16178 e^{-2.437887 \tilde{T}}\]

where \(\tilde{T} \equiv k_\mathrm{B} T/\epsilon\). Often the Lennard-Jones parameters of the two colliders are averaged together when determining the collision frequency.

epsilon¶

The depth of the potential well.

getCollisionFrequency(self, double T, double M, double mu) → double¶

Return the value of the Lennard-Jones collision frequency in Hz at the given temperature T in K for colliders with the given concentration M in mol/m^3 and reduced mass mu in amu.

sigma¶

The distance at which the inter-particle potential is minimum.
